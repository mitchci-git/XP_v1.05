About Me

I'm a graphic designer based in Brisbane, Australia, committed to continual growth and pushing my limits in the industry. I enjoy creating work that solves real problems and resonates with people, always striving to improve with each project.

Growing up in New Zealand, rugby played a big part in shaping who I am. It taught me confidence, built lasting friendships, and offered me an early outlet for creativity. Sport, particularly rugby, also gave me a firsthand look at the impact of design. In a country where the All Blacks' visual identity is everywhere, I saw how it fostered pride and unity; an influence that still shapes how I approach design today.

After rediscovering my passion for design over the course of my twenties, I decided to formalize my skills by completing QLD TAFE's Diploma of Graphic Design. This gave me a solid foundation in design principles and helped me refine my skills with tools like Adobe Creative Suite, allowing me to transform ideas into strong, visual concepts.

I'm particularly inspired by sports-related design work, where I can blend my passion for design with my love for sport. My long-term goal is to work with a major sports franchise, contributing to their brand and creative vision. For now, I'm focused on sharpening my skills and embracing any opportunity that challenges me to grow as a designer.
